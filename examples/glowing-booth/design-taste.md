# design-taste

> Claude's aesthetic compass for this brand. Every visual decision — layout, hierarchy, whitespace, color use, imagery direction — should pass through this skill first. When in doubt, return here.

**Always read `figma-design-system.md` alongside this skill.** Token values live there.

---

## Brand aesthetic identity

This is a **bold editorial brand** with a warm, high-contrast palette and a confident typographic voice. The design DNA is:

- **High contrast:** Charcoal on Vanilla. Black on Orange. Never muddy, never pastel-soft.
- **Generous scale:** Display type is large and unapologetic. Headlines dominate. White space is not a problem — it's the point.
- **Warm, not cold:** Despite being typographically rigorous, the brand is warm. Vanilla backgrounds, orange accents, analog imagery. Not a cold tech brand.
- **Precision grotesque + pixel accent:** Neue Haas Grotesk brings Swiss precision. OffBit brings personality and irreverence. The tension between them is the brand voice.
- **Analog soul:** "Reality on Film" and "Analog Details" imagery components signal that this brand values texture, imperfection, and physical warmth over sterile digital polish.

## Color philosophy

**The three-color rule:** Every surface should use at most three colors from the palette. Charcoal + Vanilla + one accent.

**Orange is earned.** It is not a background color. It is the moment of activation — a button, an underline on hover, a highlighted stat, a pull quote accent. Use it to direct the eye, not to decorate.

**Light mode default:** Vanilla (`#FFFAEE`) background with Charcoal text. This is warmer than white — it reads as considered and editorial.

**Dark mode:** Charcoal (`#191919`) background with Vanilla text and Orange accent. Feels sophisticated and brand-forward. Hero sections and campaign moments live here.

**Never use:**
- Pure white (`#FFFFFF`) as a background — use Vanilla instead
- Pure black (`#000000`) for body text — use Charcoal
- More than one brand-scale orange tone on the same surface
- Generic blues, greens, or purples — the mono and brand scales are the only ramps

## Typography philosophy

**Headlines are the design.** On this brand, typography is not supporting the layout — it is the layout. Large, bold, tightly tracked headline type should be the first thing a viewer sees and feels.

**Scale contrast is essential.** If your display type is 160px, your body should be 20px. Never compress the scale. The contrast between massive display type and intimate body text creates energy.

**Weight as voice:**
- Bold (75) = statement, authority, campaign
- Medium (65) = confident, editorial
- Roman (55) = readable, calm, informational
- Light/Thin = never for body at small sizes — use only for large display moments

**OffBit is a signal.** When OffBit appears, something interesting is happening — a number, a label, a campaign callout. It communicates: "this matters, look here." Treat it like punctuation, not prose.

**Letter spacing rules:**
- Display sizes: always negative (-2px). Never track out.
- H1–H3: slightly negative (-0.5px to -0.25px)
- Body and smaller: 0px. Never add positive tracking to text sizes.

## Layout philosophy

**Editorial grid thinking.** Content doesn't have to be symmetrical. Asymmetric layouts — a large headline dominating the left, a small caption offset bottom-right — feel more alive and designed.

**Section rhythm:** Alternate between Vanilla-background and Charcoal-background sections. This creates natural pacing, like chapters in a story.

**Generous section padding.** On desktop, sections breathe: 96px–128px vertical padding. On mobile, 48px–64px. Content should never feel squeezed.

**Max-width is 1184px on desktop.** Content stays contained. Wide layouts feel cheap.

**The 2-column split** is the workhorse layout: left column for the headline/navigation, right column for body content. Works for every section type.

## Imagery direction

Based on the Figma components, there are two approved imagery modes:

**Reality on Film:** Photographic images with the quality of analog film — grain, warmth, natural light. Not oversaturated, not perfectly composed. Feels lived-in and real.

**Analog Details:** Close-up macro shots of textures, materials, objects. Emphasizes craft, attention to detail, physical reality. Good for background imagery and section dividers.

**Never use:**
- Stock photography that looks generic
- Oversaturated or HDR-processed images
- Illustrations or cartoons (unless a deliberate campaign choice)
- Images that feel cold, corporate, or sterile

## Moodboard principles

When generating moodboards, the reference points for this brand are:

- **Typography references:** Swiss International Style, Müller-Brockmann, early Emigre
- **Brand references:** Braun, Maison Margiela, A24 film posters, Dutch graphic design
- **Digital references:** Are.na aesthetics, minimal editorial sites, linear.app precision
- **Campaign references:** Bold single-idea campaigns, poster-first thinking, type-as-image

A moodboard for this brand should feel: restrained, bold, warm, precise, considered.

---

## What this brand is not

- Not cute or playful (OffBit adds edge, not cuteness)
- Not maximalist or decorative
- Not corporate or safe
- Not cold or tech-bro minimal
- Not gradient-heavy or glassmorphism-first
